2026 Guidance
Though Apple hasn’t issued fiscal 2026 guidance, it expects revenue growth from strong iPhone and Services demand and new product launches.

CEO's Commentary
"Apple delivered a September quarter revenue record of $102.5 billion, driven by record iPhone and Services performance, said Tim Cook, CEO. With the launch of our most advanced products yet, including iPhone 17, AirPods Pro 3, and Apple Watch, alongside the new MacBook Pro and iPad Pro with the M5 chip, we’re entering the holiday season with our strongest lineup ever."

Performance Full year
• Apple achieved record fiscal 2025 revenue of $416.2 billion, up 6% YoY, driven by robust iPhone and Services sales. This marks the highest annual revenue in the company’s history, underpinned by resilient global demand and continued ecosystem expansion.
• Net sales grew across key markets: Americas +7%, Europe +10%, Japan +15%, and Rest of Asia Pacific +10%, reflecting product diversification and stable consumer spending. Only Greater China declined 4%, impacted by competitive and macro headwinds.
• Overall gross margin improved to 46.9%, aided by higher-margin Services and cost efficiencies. Services margin rose to 75.4%, offsetting mild compression in Products (-0.4 pp to 36.8%) due to tariff costs.
• Net income rose 19.5% to $112.0 billion, with EPS up 22% YoY to $7.46 diluted. This reflects operating income of $133.1 billion (+8%) and a lower effective tax rate (15.6% vs 24.1% in 2024).
• R&D spending increased 27% to $3.47 billion (19.7% of revenue), reflecting investment in late-stage assets. Positive Phase 3 results for orforglipron and FDA approval of Inluriyo mark major milestones, positioning Lilly for continued innovation-led growth. 
• Operating cash flow remained robust at $111.5 billion, supporting capital investment and shareholder returns. Apple closed the year with $132.4 billion in cash and marketable securities, demonstrating exceptional balance-sheet strength.
• Apple executed $89.3 billion in share repurchases and paid $15.4 billion in dividends. The quarterly dividend was raised to $0.26/share, and a $100 billion repurchase authorization underscores sustained capital discipline.
• Gross margin (46.91%) remained solid, supported by favorable product mix and operational efficiency, while operating margin (32.27%) and net income margin (27.21%) improved year-over-year, underscoring disciplined cost management and revenue leverage.

Highlights
• iPhone revenue rose 4% to $209.6 billion, led by strong demand for premium iPhone 17 Pro and Pro Max models. The product’s performance was especially strong in the Americas and Japan.
• Mac sales rose 12% to $33.7 billion, supported by strong uptake of M5-powered laptops and desktops. Favorable product mix and corporate demand recovery contributed to the double-digit growth.
• iPad revenue climbed 5% to $28.0 billion, reversing last year’s decline. Gains stemmed from robust demand for iPad Air and iPad mini, offsetting slightly weaker Pro model performance.
• Services revenue surged 14% to $109.2 billion, setting an all-time high. Growth was driven by advertising, the App Store, and cloud services, expanding high-margin recurring income that now accounts for over 26% of total revenue.
